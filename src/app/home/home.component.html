Mission statement

Since the beginning of the 21st century, Western governments and corporations have normalised the ubiquitous surveilance of their own law-abiding populations, motivated by financial gain and social control.

CCTV and number-plate recognition already dominate our urban centres, while facial recognition without consent is creeping into public spaces. Such technology is used heavily in areas with ethnic minority and migrant populations, under the guise of tackling crime. The over-policing of marginalised groups only entrenches social inequality, especially when such tactics depend on so called `artificial intelligence` which has been shown to reflect the biases of (usually white) software developers. In cities like London, with both dense CCTV coverage and digital-only ticketing for public transport, it is becoming rapidly impossible to avoid continuous tracking by the aparatus of an omnipresent state.

In the digital world, companies like Google and Facebook track each user across the internet, observing and recording every minute detail of online behaviour. This data is collected into a digital profile: a comprehensive map of an individual's innermost thoughts and desires, assembled without their knowlege or approval, for sale to the highest bidder. Buyers include political campaign groups who direct targeted messaging to spread disinformation and propaghanda on a massive scale. States also exploit these enourmous corporate data caches, with indescriminate access to private data granted in the name of national security.

Let's turn the tables.
